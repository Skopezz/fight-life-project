Classes

BJJ

Brazilian Jiu Jitsu is a martial art and a combat sport that originated from the famous Gracie family in Brazil, as such, it is often called Gracie Jiu Jitsu. As a sport, the main objective of a Jiu Jitsu fighter is to force his opponent to give up (called a submission or a tap-out) by using joint locks or chokes, a fighter can also win by points, the point system is designed to reward the most dominant fighter. Brazilian Jiu Jitsu is revered as one of the most effective martial arts in the world

Boxing

The UK is rich in Boxing history and pedigree. One of the oldest recognised forms of combat, boxing is the refined art striking with the fists. Boxing serves as a great sport to reach all kinds of personal goals; whether its for fitness, self defence, competition or simply being a fan of “the sweet science”.

Wrestling

Wrestling, a fundamental component of mixed martial arts (MMA), is a dynamic and physically demanding sport that emphasizes skill, technique, and strength. Rooted in ancient history and adapted for modern competition, wrestling serves as a cornerstone for fighters seeking success in the diverse world of MMA. Wrestling in MMA involves a blend of grappling, takedowns, and ground control, making it an essential discipline for fighters aiming to dominate their opponents in various positions. Wrestlers are adept at utilizing their body positioning, leverage, and superior conditioning to dictate the pace of a bout and secure advantageous positions

Muay Thai

Muay Thai or Thai Boxing, is one of the most widely respected forms of stand up fighting (also known as “striking”) in the world. Unlike in boxing, Muay Thai fighters can make full use of punches, elbows, kicks, knees and clinch work to neutralise their opponents. As well as a very effective fighting style Muay Thai is a lot of fun and a tremendous work-out.

No Gi

No Gi is the practice of BJJ without wearing the kimono (Gi). It is the fastest growing form of grappling in the world, with the ADCC ruleset becoming the most dominant format for No Gi competition.

Yoga

The beauty of yoga is that you don’t have to be a yogi or yogini to reap the benefits. Whether you are young or old, yoga has the power to calm the mind and strengthen the body. At Fight Life, we practice Vinyasa yoga. Vinyasa is an approach to yoga in which you move from one pose directly into the next. There's a flow to a Vinyasa yoga session.
